Promptster is a lightweight CLI that runs alongside your AI coding tool during a technical assessment. It captures a record of your session — the prompts you write, the changes the AI makes, the commands you run, and the decisions you explain — so the hiring team can evaluate *how you work*, not just the code you submit.

You install one binary, redeem a key, and work normally. Everything else happens in the background.

## You're judged on how you use AI, not whether you use it

Final code output is a weak signal on its own. Two candidates can ship the same diff through completely different processes — one who understood every line and verified it, one who pasted whatever the model produced. Promptster gives the hiring team the evidence trail to tell those apart: how you decompose a problem, what you verify, where you push back on the AI, and why you made the tradeoffs you did.

Use AI as much or as little as you like. There's no penalty for leaning on it heavily and no bonus for avoiding it. The signal is *judgment* — the quality of your direction and your verification.

## What the experience is like

You work the way you normally would:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install and start">
    Install the CLI and run `promptster start PST-XXXX-XXXX`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build">
    Open **Claude Code**, **Codex**, or **Cursor** from your assessment workspace and solve the task.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain your decisions">
    When you make a meaningful choice, run `/explain` (in Claude Code) or `promptster explain` to record your reasoning in your own words.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Run `promptster done`. You get a link to your own session replay.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The CLI captures telemetry quietly while you work — you don't interact with it between those steps. The one habit worth building is **explaining your decisions**: a sentence on *why* you chose an approach is one of the highest-signal things a reviewer sees.

<Note>
  Most assessments run in **managed** mode: your AI tool is routed through Promptster, **the hiring team covers the cost, not you**, and you don't need a paid plan or to log out of a personal one. Some assessments have you use your own subscription instead — `promptster start` sets up whichever the assessment uses. See [Signing in to the AI](/candidates/troubleshooting#signing-in-to-the-ai).
</Note>

## Key concepts

| Term | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Assessment key** | A one-time code in `PST-XXXX-XXXX` format, emailed to you by the hiring team. You redeem it with `promptster start`. |
| **Workspace** | The directory where you do the assessment. Your tool's hooks (and, in managed mode, the AI proxy) are scoped to this folder — always open your tool *from here*. |
| **Session** | The capture window, from `promptster start` until `promptster done` (or until the time limit runs out). |
| **Decision** | An architecture or tradeoff choice you document with `/explain`. Reviewers read these alongside your code. |
| **Task brief** | What you're being asked to build — shown by `promptster brief` and saved to `TASK.md` in your workspace. |

## What is and isn't recorded

Promptster only records activity inside your assessment workspace and your AI coding tool. It does **not** record keystrokes, screen contents, your clipboard, browser activity, environment variables, or files outside the workspace.

See [Privacy & Data](/candidates/privacy) for the complete list, including the optional authorship-timing layer you can decline at consent.
