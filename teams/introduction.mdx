Promptster for Teams is a lightweight CLI that captures how your engineers actually work with AI coding tools — the prompts they write, the decisions they make, and the metadata around them — so your team gets an honest picture of AI adoption and impact. It runs on each engineer's own machine, reads the transcripts your AI tools already write to disk, and streams a **redacted** record to your team's Promptster dashboard.

The load-bearing promise: **Promptster for Teams never stores your source code.** Source, diffs, file contents, command output, and AI responses are stripped on your machine — before anything is written to disk, signed, or sent. This isn't a policy you have to trust; it's how the tool is built.

<Note>
  This is a different product from Promptster's hiring assessments. The Teams CLI has no assessment, scoring-against-you, or anti-cheat logic. It measures adoption and impact for a team you're already on — not candidates. It also uses its own binary (`promptster-teams`) and its own local directory (`~/.promptster-teams`), so it coexists cleanly with the hiring CLI if you have both.
</Note>

## Who runs what

<Steps>
  <Step title="An admin mints your key">
    A manager or admin creates a per-developer key in your team's Promptster dashboard and sends it to you. Keys look like `PSE-XXXX-XXXX`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You connect once">
    You install the CLI and run `promptster-teams login`. That saves your key and starts capture in the background. You don't run anything again day to day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture runs quietly">
    As you use **Claude Code** or **Codex**, the CLI tails their transcripts, redacts secrets and strips source locally, and ships the redacted metadata to the dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What it captures

Promptster for Teams records the *shape* of your AI-assisted work, never its content:

- The prompts you send to the AI
- Tool-call metadata — file paths and line counts for edits, commands run (with any inline code masked) and their exit codes
- Per-request token usage and the exact model, so your team can see cost
- Timestamps

It does **not** capture your source code, diffs, file contents, command output, AI responses, keystrokes, your screen, your clipboard, secrets, or your identity. Events carry an anonymous per-device hash, not your name or email. For the complete list and how it's enforced, see [Privacy & security](/teams/privacy).

## Supported tools

The CLI captures **Claude Code** and **Codex** by reading the transcript files they write to disk. Both must be installed and have run at least once so their transcript directories exist. Cursor is not supported.
